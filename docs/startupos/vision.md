# StartupOS — Vision & Purpose

## The problem StartupOS solves

Praxis is a strong engine for turning a *defined* idea into well-built software. But it assumes the idea is worth building. In reality, most ideas should die early: they address no real pain, target no one who pays, or have no defensibility. Building them is expensive failure.

There is no equivalent rigor *before* the build. Founders jump from a hunch to a backlog, skipping discovery, evidence, validation, and honest financial modeling. AI makes this worse: it will happily generate a confident-sounding TAM and a glossy business plan out of thin air.

**StartupOS is the disciplined front door.** It is an AI-native operating system for the pre-build phase — discovering many candidate opportunities, killing the weak ones, validating the survivors with cheap experiments, designing the business and the product, and only then handing a validated, Praxis-ready project to Praxis.

## Vision

> A world where every product Praxis builds is one that *should* exist — backed by real pain, real evidence, and a real business model — because StartupOS proved it first.

StartupOS treats idea selection as an engineering discipline: hypotheses, experiments, evidence, gates, and traceable decisions — the same rigor Praxis brings to code, applied to *whether to build at all*.

## Purpose

1. **Generate optionality.** Discover many candidate opportunities rather than fixating on the first idea.
2. **Separate signal from story.** Force a clean split between **facts, assumptions, estimates, and hypotheses** — and never invent market data.
3. **Kill weak ideas cheaply.** Red-team and reject before money is spent.
4. **Validate demand before supply.** Design falsifiable experiments; require evidence before selection.
5. **Design the company, not just the app.** Business model, pricing, unit economics, GTM, and risk — not only features.
6. **Hand off cleanly.** Produce a self-contained, Praxis-ready bundle so engineering starts with context, not a blank page.

## What StartupOS is *not*

- It is **not** a replacement for Praxis. It precedes Praxis and ends by feeding it.
- It is **not** a product or app repo — like Praxis, it produces *artifacts and decisions*, not production code.
- It is **not** a fantasy-generator. It refuses to fabricate numbers; unsupported claims are labeled assumptions.
- It does **not** build product. Its terminal action is `/startupos-export-praxis`.

## Design principles

- **Reuse Praxis conventions.** Claude Code-native commands, markdown templates, a memory ledger mindset, and human-in-the-loop gates — the same patterns Praxis already uses, so the two feel like one system.
- **Evidence over eloquence.** A well-labeled "we don't know yet" beats a confident fabrication.
- **Human-in-the-loop where it matters.** The two highest-stakes decisions — *which idea* and *whether to hand off* — require explicit human approval.
- **Bias toward killing.** Saying no early is the cheapest decision a founder ever makes.
- **AI as leverage, not as oracle.** AI accelerates research, drafting, and critique — but the human owns selection and export.

## How it fits the Praxis ecosystem

StartupOS sits *above* Praxis in the value chain:

```
  idea space ──▶ StartupOS ──(validated, Praxis-ready project)──▶ Praxis ──▶ shipped product
   (chaos)      what/why/who                 handoff gate            how              value
```

Both halves share one philosophy: **understand before acting, validate before committing, and leave a traceable record of every decision.**
