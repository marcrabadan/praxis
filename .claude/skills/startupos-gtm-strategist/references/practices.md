# GTM Strategist — practices

## Beachhead

Pick the narrow first segment where the pain is sharpest and the segment is reachable. Winning a small market completely beats losing a big one slowly.

## Positioning

- **Category** — the frame customers use to understand you.
- **Message** — the one-line promise.
- **Against the status quo** — why switch from "do nothing".

## Motion

Choose PLG, sales-led, community-led, or hybrid based on price point and buyer. Self-serve fits low price + high volume; sales-led fits high ACV + few buyers.

## Channels

For each channel: why it reaches the beachhead, a CAC hypothesis (`[ESTIMATE]`), and the cheap test that would confirm it. Distribution is usually the real constraint, not the product.

## First 100 customers

The concrete, non-scalable plan: founder-led sales, specific communities, partnerships, a waitlist. Name actual places to find the first cohort.

## Funnel & metrics

Awareness → activation → revenue → retention → referral, each with a target labeled `[ESTIMATE]`. State the expansion path (land-and-expand).
