# Market Analyst — practices

## Building the thesis

One paragraph: who the buyer is, the job-to-be-done, and why the market is attractive *now*. Everything else supports or qualifies this.

## Sizing (ranges + method)

- **Bottom-up (preferred):** # of target accounts × ACV. Show the count source and the price assumption.
- **Top-down (sanity check only):** total market × a defensible capture %.
- Present TAM / SAM / SOM as **ranges**, each labeled `[ESTIMATE]` with the derivation. Never a single confident number.

## Demand evidence

Search volume, communities, existing spend, analog/adjacent products, hiring signals. Each labeled and dated. Prefer signals of *existing spend* over stated interest.

## Trends & why-now

Tailwinds and headwinds with dates and labels. Tie the "why now" to a concrete unlock (technology/AI, regulation, behavior, cost curve).

## Evidence log

One row per source: the claim it supports, the source, the date, and a confidence level. A `[FACT]` with no source is an `[ASSUMPTION]` in disguise.
