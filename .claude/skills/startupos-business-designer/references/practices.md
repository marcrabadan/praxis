# Business Designer — practices

## Value proposition

State the gain created and pain relieved, and why it beats the status quo (including "do nothing"). Tie it to the job-to-be-done.

## Business model

Choose the model (SaaS, usage-based, marketplace, transactional, hybrid) and justify it against the buyer's mental model and the cost structure. Prefer recurring revenue and existing budgets.

## The wedge

Pick the single capability that delivers value fastest and earns the right to expand. The wedge is narrow on purpose; the platform comes later.

## Packaging logic

Map tiers to customer segments and to the value metric. Keep the free/trial boundary aligned with the "aha" moment.

## Defensibility

Name the moat thesis — data, network effects, switching costs, brand, regulatory, or speed — and the evidence it can form. A model with no moat is a feature, not a company.

## Make-or-break

Identify the single assumption that, if false, sinks the business, and route it to validation.
