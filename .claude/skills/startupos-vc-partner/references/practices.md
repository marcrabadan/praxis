# VC Partner Agent — practices

## Attack vectors (work each one)

- **No real pain** — is the problem intense and frequent, or a vitamin?
- **Won't pay** — does the pain map to existing budget / spend?
- **No distribution** — is the segment reachable at a sane CAC?
- **No moat** — what stops a funded competitor or incumbent from copying it?
- **Commodity AI** — is the AI leverage durable or a thin wrapper over a model anyone can call?
- **Bad timing** — why now, and why not 2 years ago or 2 years from now?
- **Broken economics** — do the unit economics ever close?
- **Regulatory / legal** — is there a blocker that changes the model?

## Exposing hidden assumptions

Name the optimistic leaps stated as if they were facts; promote each to the hypothesis log with a test.

## Scoring rubric (1–5 per criterion, weights explicit)

Real pain · existing spend · recurring-revenue potential · AI leverage · defensibility · feasibility · **evidence strength** (penalize thin evidence). Show the weighted math; flag any score driven by assumption with a confidence level.

## Verdict

- **STRENGTHEN** — fixable weaknesses; list the concrete fixes.
- **PIVOT** — good idea, wrong framing/segment/model.
- **KILL** — fails the floor on pain, spend, or evidence. A kill is a win.

## Discipline

Do not invent disconfirming "data" either — critique from evidence and labeled reasoning, not fabricated counter-numbers.
