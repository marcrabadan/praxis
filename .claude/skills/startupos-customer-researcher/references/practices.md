# Customer Researcher — practices

## Segmentation & ICP

- Segment by the *job* and the *pain intensity*, not just firmographics.
- Pick the beachhead: where the pain is sharpest **and** the segment is reachable.
- Define the ICP concretely enough that you could name 10 real examples.

## Jobs-to-be-done

State the job as: *when `<situation>`, I want to `<motivation>`, so I can `<outcome>`.* Capture the functional, emotional, and social dimensions.

## Pain qualification

Rate intensity × frequency. A high-intensity, high-frequency pain with existing spend is the prize; a low-frequency nice-to-have is a trap.

## Interview design

- Ask about past behavior, not hypothetical futures ("tell me about the last time…").
- Avoid leading questions; never pitch during discovery.
- Pre-commit what answer would confirm vs. disconfirm the hypothesis.

## Synthesis (no invention)

- Quote real people; if you have no interviews yet, say so — do not fabricate quotes or personas.
- Separate what users *said* (`[FACT]` with source) from what you *infer* (`[ASSUMPTION]`).
- Surface disconfirming signals as prominently as confirming ones.
